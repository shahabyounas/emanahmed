First Author 2024

Mapping biomaterial complexity by machine learning

Ahmed, Eman, et al.

Tissue Engineering Part A, 30(19-20), 662-680

This study presents a novel machine learning approach for mapping the complexity of biomaterials, enabling predictive modeling of material properties and biological responses. Our methodology demonstrates significant improvements in understanding structure-function relationships in complex biomaterial systems.

We developed computational frameworks that integrate high-dimensional material characterization data with biological outcome measurements, providing insights that accelerate the design-build-test-learn cycle in biomaterials research.

Co-Author 2025

Automation-Assisted Photoinduced Atom Transfer Radical Polymerization

Ramirez, Cesar; Ahmed, Eman, et al.

ACS Polymers Au

We present an automated approach to photoinduced atom transfer radical polymerization (photoATRP), enabling high-throughput synthesis and characterization of polymer libraries for biomaterial applications. This methodology significantly accelerates the discovery and optimization of functional polymers by leveraging liquid handling robots and automated analytical platforms.

Co-Author 2025

SAXS Assistant: Automated SAXS analysis for structural discovery in biologics and polymeric nanoparticles

Ramirez, Cesar; ... Ahmed, Eman, et al.

Biophysical Journal, 124(21), 3772-3786

SAXS Assistant is an automated analysis pipeline for Small-Angle X-ray Scattering (SAXS) data, designed to accelerate structural discovery in biologics and polymeric nanoparticles. The tool integrates machine learning algorithms with traditional SAXS analysis methods to provide rapid, reproducible structural characterization.
